Goulash

Goulash is a soup or stew of meat and vegetables seasoned with paprika[1] and other spices.[2] Originating in Hungary, goulash is a common meal predominantly eaten in Central Europe but also in other parts of Europe. It is one of the national dishes of Hungary and a symbol of the country.[
INGREDIENTS: 2 tbsp. extra-virgin olive oil 1 medium yellow onion, chopped 2 cloves garlic, finely chopped 1 lb. ground beef Kosher salt Freshly ground black pepper 1 tbsp. tomato paste 1 (15-oz.) can diced tomatoes 1 (15-oz.) can tomato sauce 1 1/4 c. low-sodium beef broth 1 tsp. Italian seasoning 1 tsp. paprika 1 1/2 c. elbow macaroni 1 c. shredded cheddar Chopped fresh parsley, for serving
COOKING: Step 1 In a large skillet over medium heat, heat oil. Add onion and cook, stirring occasionally, until softened, about 7 minutes. Add garlic and cook, stirring, until fragrant, about 1 minute more. Step 2 Add ground beef, season with salt and pepper, and cook, breaking up with a spoon, until no longer pink, about 6 minutes. Drain fat. Step 3 Add tomato paste and stir to coat, then pour in diced tomatoes, tomato sauce, and broth. Add Italian seasoning and paprika, then stir in macaroni. Bring to a simmer and cook, stirring occasionally, until pasta is tender, 10-15 minutes. Step 4 Stir in cheese until melted; season with salt and pepper, if needed. Remove from heat. Top with parsley.
